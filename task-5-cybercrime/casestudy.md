Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI/Online Payment Fraud is a common cybercrime in which attackers trick users into transferring money using digital payment apps. This type of fraud is increasing rapidly in India due to the widespread use of UPI platforms.

A typical fraud begins when a victim receives a phone call, SMS, or message pretending to be from a bank official, delivery agent, or customer care executive. The attacker creates urgency by claiming that the victim’s account will be blocked or that a refund is pending. They then send a fake payment request or a malicious link. The victim is instructed to approve the request or enter sensitive details such as UPI PIN or OTP. Once the victim authorizes the transaction, money is instantly transferred to the attacker’s account.

Students, elderly people, and those unfamiliar with digital payments are often targeted. Lack of awareness and panic make them easy victims. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes identity misuse.

In many reported cases in India, victims lost thousands of rupees within minutes. Since UPI transactions are instant, recovery is difficult.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and cautious behavior while using digital payment platforms.
