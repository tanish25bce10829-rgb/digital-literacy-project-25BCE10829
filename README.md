Task 1: Digital Literacy Infographic

In this task, I created a one-page infographic to explain the concept of digital literacy in a simple and engaging way. I used Canva as my design tool because it provides easy templates and icons. My infographic included topics like what digital literacy means, useful tools for students, safe internet practices, and email etiquette. While designing, I focused on keeping the content short and visually appealing so that it is easy for students to understand at a glance. One challenge I faced was arranging all the information in a limited space without making it look cluttered. However, this also helped me improve my design and presentation skills. Overall, this task made me realize how important it is to communicate information clearly using visuals.

Task 2: Student Digital Portfolio

In this task, I worked on building my digital presence by creating and updating profiles on platforms like GitHub and LinkedIn. I created a GitHub profile README with my basic details such as name, branch, and learning goals. On LinkedIn, I added my education details and made sure my profile looked professional. This task helped me understand the importance of having an online presence, especially for internships and future career opportunities. I also learned how different platforms serve different purposes, such as GitHub for coding projects and LinkedIn for networking. It made me think about how I will use these platforms in the coming years to showcase my skills and achievements. Overall, this task was very useful for building a strong digital identity.

Task 3: Coding & Collaboration Platforms

In this task, I explored both coding and collaboration tools. I created an account on HackerRank and solved a beginner-level problem, which helped me understand basic programming logic and improve my problem-solving skills. It was interesting to apply simple logic and see the output working correctly. In the second part, I created a Google Form titled “Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz” with five questions. I included both multiple-choice and short-answer questions. I also linked the form to Google Sheets to automatically collect responses. This helped me understand how data can be collected and managed efficiently. This task was practical and gave me exposure to tools that are widely used in academics and teamwork. It also increased my confidence in using online platforms effectively.

Task 4: Email Etiquette & Communication

In this task, I learned how to write professional emails and maintain proper communication online. I wrote two emails: one requesting an assignment deadline extension and another expressing interest in an internship opportunity. While writing these emails, I focused on using a clear subject line, proper greeting, polite tone, and structured content. I also created a checklist of social media do’s and don’ts to promote responsible online behavior. This task helped me understand how small mistakes in communication can create a negative impression. I realized the importance of being clear, respectful, and professional while writing emails. Overall, this task improved my communication skills and prepared me for real-life academic and professional interactions.

Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness

In this task, I explored cybercrime, focusing on UPI and online payment fraud. I wrote a case study explaining how such frauds happen step by step, who is usually targeted, and what consequences victims face. While researching, I was surprised by how easily people can be tricked through fake calls, messages, and links. I also created a prevention checklist with practical tips that students can follow to stay safe online. This task made me more aware of digital threats and the importance of being cautious while using online platforms. One important habit I will follow is never sharing sensitive information like OTP or UPI PIN. Overall, this task was very informative and helped me understand how to protect myself in the digital world.
